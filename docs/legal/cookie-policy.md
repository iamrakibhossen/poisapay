# Cookie Policy

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 About this Policy
This Cookie Policy explains how PoisaPay ("we", "us", "our") uses cookies, local storage, and similar technologies on the PoisaPay platform, web application, and related services (the "Services"). It should be read together with our [Privacy Policy](privacy-policy.md).

### 1.2 Effective Date
This Policy is effective as of [PLACEHOLDER: effective date] and may be updated as described in Section 8.

### 1.3 What Are Cookies and Similar Technologies
Cookies are small text files placed on your device by a website. Local storage is a browser mechanism that stores data on your device. Similar technologies include tokens and identifiers used to operate and secure the Services. We refer to these collectively as "cookies" in this Policy unless stated otherwise.

## 2. How We Use Cookies

The Services are server-rendered (traditional Blade MVC) and use cookies primarily to operate, secure, and personalise your experience. Specifically, we use:

### 2.1 Authentication and Session
A session cookie to keep you logged in and maintain your authenticated session across pages.

### 2.2 Security and Anti-Fraud
A cross-site request forgery (CSRF) token to protect form submissions, and security-related cookies and signals used for suspicious-login detection, device management, and anti-fraud protection.

### 2.3 Preferences
Preference cookies to remember choices such as language, display currency (for example [PLACEHOLDER: BDT/USD]), and interface theme.

### 2.4 Local Storage for UI
Limited local storage used by standalone client-side components (Alpine) to support light user-interface functionality, such as remembering the state of interface elements.

## 3. Categories of Cookies

### 3.1 Strictly Necessary
These cookies are essential for the Services to function and cannot be switched off in our systems. They include the session cookie, the CSRF token, and core security cookies. Without these, core features such as logging in and submitting forms will not work.

### 3.2 Functional
These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalisation, such as remembering your language, display currency, and theme preferences. If you disable them, some features may not work as intended.

### 3.3 Analytics
These cookies help us understand how the Services are used so we can improve them. Analytics may be disabled by default and, where enabled, will be subject to your consent where required [PLACEHOLDER: analytics provider / status].

## 4. Consent

### 4.1 Necessary Cookies
Strictly necessary cookies are exempt from consent because they are required to provide the Services you request.

### 4.2 Non-Necessary Cookies
Functional and analytics cookies that are not strictly necessary are set only with your consent where required by applicable law. Where a consent mechanism is presented, you may accept or decline non-necessary categories and change your choices at any time.

### 4.3 Withdrawing Consent
You may withdraw consent for non-necessary cookies at any time through the cookie controls provided in the Services (where available) or your browser settings, as described in Section 6.

## 6. Managing and Disabling Cookies

### 6.1 Browser Controls
Most browsers let you view, manage, block, and delete cookies through their settings. Instructions vary by browser; consult your browser's help documentation for details.

### 6.2 Consequences of Disabling
If you block or delete strictly necessary cookies, core features such as login, secure form submission, and session continuity may stop working. Disabling functional cookies may reset preferences such as language, currency, and theme.

### 6.3 Local Storage
You can clear local storage through your browser's site-data controls. Clearing it may reset interface state maintained by client-side components.

## 7. Personal Data and Cookies
