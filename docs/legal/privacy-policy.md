## 3. Categories of Personal Data We Collect

We collect and process the following categories of personal data.

### 3.1 Identity Data
Full legal name, date of birth, nationality, gender (where required), national identifier or tax identifier, and other identity attributes required to establish your identity under applicable law.

### 3.2 KYC and Verification Data
Government-issued identity documents (such as passport, national ID, or driving licence), document images, selfie images, and liveness/biometric-derived verification signals collected during onboarding and ongoing due diligence. Verification is performed through our identity verification vendor (see Section 6).

### 3.3 Contact Data
Email address, phone number, and postal or residential address, including any address used for physical card fulfilment.

### 3.4 Device, Network, and Location Data
Device identifiers, browser and operating system information, IP address, approximate geolocation derived from IP, session identifiers, and security signals used for suspicious-login and device-management features.

### 3.5 Transaction and Account Data
Records of deposits, on-chain withdrawals, internal transfers, currency swaps (including applied spread), merchant payments, QR invoices, settlements, rewards, referrals, and credit-line activity, together with associated amounts, timestamps, counterparties, and statuses. Base and display currencies are [PLACEHOLDER: BDT/USD] as applicable.

### 3.6 On-Chain Data
Blockchain wallet addresses associated with your account, deposit and withdrawal addresses, transaction hashes, and related on-chain metadata for supported networks (USDT on TRON (TRC20) and on Ethereum and BSC (ERC20)).

### 3.7 Financial and Payment Rail Data
Bank account details and mobile-wallet details (for example bKash and Nagad) used for fiat on-ramp and off-ramp, and related payment instructions and settlement records processed through payment service providers.

### 3.8 Card Data
Card issuance and usage data for virtual and physical cards, processed through our card program manager and issuer. Sensitive card credentials (such as full card numbers and security codes) are handled by the card program manager and issuer in accordance with applicable card-scheme and security standards; we receive limited card metadata as required to operate the Services.

### 3.9 Cookies and Usage Data
Cookies, local storage, and usage information as described in our [Cookie Policy](cookie-policy.md).

### 3.10 Communications and Support Data
Records of your communications with us, support requests, and correspondence, including anti-phishing preferences.

## 4. Purposes of Processing and Legal Bases

We process personal data for the purposes set out below. Where applicable law follows a GDPR-style framework, the legal bases in Article 6 style are indicated. Your local law may characterise these bases differently.

### 4.1 Providing the Services (Contract — Art. 6(1)(b))
To create and administer your account and custodial wallet; to process deposits, withdrawals, transfers, swaps, merchant payments, settlements, card transactions, rewards, referrals, and credit lines; and to provide customer support.

### 4.2 Identity Verification, KYC, and AML (Legal Obligation — Art. 6(1)(c))
To perform customer due diligence and enhanced due diligence, apply KYC tiers (0, 1, and 2), conduct sanctions and politically-exposed-person (PEP) screening, comply with anti-money-laundering and counter-terrorist-financing obligations, meet record-keeping and reporting duties, and respond to lawful requests from competent authorities.

### 4.3 Fraud Prevention and Security (Legitimate Interests — Art. 6(1)(f))
To detect, prevent, and investigate fraud, money laundering, abuse, and security incidents; to operate suspicious-login detection, device management, blockchain analytics (KYT) monitoring, risk scoring, and rate limiting; and to protect the integrity of the Services. Our legitimate interest is maintaining a secure and compliant platform, balanced against your rights and interests.

### 4.4 Legal and Regulatory Compliance (Legal Obligation — Art. 6(1)(c))
To comply with tax, accounting, dispute-resolution, and other legal obligations, and to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims.

### 4.5 Service Improvement and Analytics (Legitimate Interests — Art. 6(1)(f))
To maintain, improve, and secure the Services, understand usage, and develop new features, using aggregated or limited data where practicable.

### 4.6 Marketing and Communications (Consent — Art. 6(1)(a))
To send promotional communications where you have consented. You may withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
