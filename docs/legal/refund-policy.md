## 1. Nature of Crypto and Stablecoin Transactions

1.1. **Finality of on-chain transactions.** Transfers of digital assets (including USDT on TRON/TRC20 and on Ethereum/BNB Smart Chain/ERC20) that are broadcast to and settled on a public blockchain are, by their nature, final and irreversible. Once an On-Chain Transaction is confirmed, PoisaPay cannot recall, cancel, reverse, or refund it.

1.2. **Internal transfers.** Internal Transfers between PoisaPay Accounts settle on PoisaPay's internal ledger and are generally final once completed. Reversal may be possible only in limited circumstances described in Section 2 and only before final settlement.

1.3. **Exchanges and swaps.** Currency exchanges and swaps are executed at a rate that includes a spread. Once executed, an exchange or swap is final and is not refundable due to subsequent rate movements.

1.4. **User responsibility.** You are solely responsible for the accuracy of transaction details, including destination address, network, asset, and amount. PoisaPay is not able to recover assets sent to an incorrect address, on an unsupported network, or in an unsupported asset.

## 2. When Refunds or Reversals May Apply

2.1. Refunds or reversals are exceptional and are only considered in the following circumstances, subject to verification and applicable law:

2.1.1. **Failed off-ramp.** Where a fiat off-ramp (crypto to fiat over bank transfer or mobile wallet such as bKash or Nagad) fails, is rejected, or is returned by the receiving rail, the underlying amount (net of any non-refundable fees under Section 4) may be credited back to your Account balance.

2.1.2. **Failed on-ramp.** Where a fiat on-ramp is charged but the corresponding crypto credit is not completed due to a PoisaPay-side error, we will credit the corresponding amount or reverse the charge, subject to reconciliation with the payment rail.

2.1.3. **Duplicate charge.** Where you are charged more than once for the same transaction due to a technical error, the duplicate amount will be refunded following verification.

2.1.4. **Processing error attributable to PoisaPay.** Where an amount was debited in error due to a demonstrable fault on our side, we will correct the error.

2.1.5. **Card transactions.** Where a Card transaction is eligible for dispute or chargeback under Section 3.

2.2. A refund does not guarantee restoration of any lost value arising from exchange-rate movements, network fees already incurred, or third-party rail fees.

2.3. We may require identity verification, transaction evidence, and supporting documentation before processing any refund, and may decline requests that fail verification or that we reasonably believe are fraudulent or abusive.
